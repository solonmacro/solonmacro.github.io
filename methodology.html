Methodology

SolonInsight is designed to track systemic risk using a small set of high-signal indicators. The goal is not to predict exact market tops or bottoms, but to identify when risk conditions are improving or deteriorating in ways that have historically mattered for financial stability.

Core Principles

  • Focus on leading indicators: credit conditions and liquidity tend to move before equities.
  • Prefer changes over levels: trends and inflections often matter more than absolute values.
  • Use simple, explicit rules: thresholds are transparent and can be independently verified.
  • Separate signal from noise: the dashboard is updated on schedules that match each dataset (daily/weekly/monthly).

Indicator Groups

1) Credit & Funding Conditions (Highest Priority)

Credit markets often price stress earlier than equity indices. Widening spreads, tightening lending standards, and signs of refinancing pressure can indicate that the marginal borrower is under strain.

2) Labor Market

The labor market influences consumption and default risk. Inflections in unemployment or jobless claims can signal that economic momentum is weakening. Labor indicators can be lagging, so they are interpreted together with credit conditions.
